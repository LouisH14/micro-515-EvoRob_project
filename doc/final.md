**Introduction**  
For the final report we are now interested in designing an evolutionary experiment ourselves. The goal of the experiment is to evolve a robot that can walk in positive x-direction in four challenging environments (flat, ice, hill and mystery). Before just running some code, think clearly how you want to tackle this task. Some possible suggestions to consider:
 
- Different types of tasks  
- Different geno2opheno mappings/representations  
- Different types of controllers  
- Body evolution \+ lifetime learning

For the sake of brevity, limit yourself to only one domain of interest (e.g. a custom controller). Hypothesize what differences you would expect in terms of body (morphological design) and brain (robot control strategies) between the different conditions.
Ask questions for guidance when stuck!
